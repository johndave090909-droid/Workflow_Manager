The Ground Oven

Ground ovens are traditional throughout the Polynesian Islands. In the early morning hours at the Center a Hawaiian ground oven, or imu, is built and used to dry age and steam a whole roasted pig for 10 hours to ensure the most tender and delicious meat. This practice has been passed down through the generations — honoring the long-standing traditions of Polynesian cooking.

Cooking on Hot Rocks

Cultural experts from our Samoan village and Executive Chef Felix Tai showcase one of the many ways that the Center uses traditional Polynesian techniques and recipes in innovative ways to bring unique dishes to our Ohana. Our chefs use a hot rock technique to create a delicious meal with fresh Kona Kampachi fish marinated in brown sugar and coconut milk vinaigrette — based off a traditional Samoan recipe.
